What is "The Game"?

"The Game" is a popular mind game where the only objective is to not think of it. There are only 3 rules:

  1. Rule #1: Everyone in the world is playing The Game. You cannot refuse to play The Game; it does not require consent to play and you can never stop playing.

  2. Rule #2: Whenever you think of The Game, you lose.

  3. Rule #3: You must announce when you lose. This could be verbally or on social media.

  4. A website about The Game:

    losethegame.com

Finchley Central

Similar to "The Game", Finchley Central is also a mind game where two players take turns naming stations in London Underground.


The way you win is by being the first person to say the station "Finchley Central"


It may seem like the obvious way to win is to say "Finchley Central" as your first move but it may deprive you of a more satisfying win down the line.

The Button

While not necessarily an Internet game, The Button was a reddit social experiment created by Josh Wardle on April 1, 2015.

There was a big red button with a 60 second timer counting down on it at the top of the reddit subreddit. Everytime you press the button, the timer would reset.

The social experiement finally ended on June 5, 2015 when the button finally ticked down to 0 without any presses. The final presser was reddit user, BigGoron who was dubbed "The Pressiah".

The experiment ended with 1,008,316 logged button clicks.

Rickroll

The infamous rickroll actually originated from 4Chan. Someone created a filter that changed the word egg to duck, so when someone typed eggroll, it would change to duckroll.

Someone thought it was funny so they made an image of a duck with wheels. It became a trend to make posts that just linked to the duck with wheels which was dubbed as duckroll

-rolling becomes a suffix that means bait and switch prank. Then in 2007, someone posted a link disguised as another video to Rick Astley's, "Never Gonna Give You Up"

It becomes a trend and rickrolling becomes one of the most infamous memes ever.
